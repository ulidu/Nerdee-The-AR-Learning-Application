Some cool facts about skeleton

  •   Bones are filled with a spongy tissue

  • Bone marrow is a spongy substance that’s found inside large bones like your hips, pelvis, and femur. Bone marrow houses stem cells. Stem cells are responsible for producing many of your body’s most important cells, including blood, brain, heart, and bone cells.

  •   The smallest bone in the body is in your ear

  • The stapes, a bone in your inner ear, is the smallestTrusted Source of all your bones. This bone is also sometimes called the stirrup because of its Y shape. Together with the anvil and hammer bones, the stapes helps translate sounds you hear into waves your brain can understand.

  •   The longest bone in the body is in your leg

  • The femur, which runs from your hip to your knee, is the longest and largest bone in your body. It’s also the strongest. Just think of all the weight that bone handles in a day. No wonder it’s so strong!

  •   Some people have an extra rib that can cause health issues.

  • Most adults have 24 ribs (12 pairs), but about one in every 500 people has an extra rib, called a cervical rib. This rib, which grows from the base of the neck just above the collarbone, is not always fully formed. It's sometimes just a thin strand of tissue fibers.

  •   Babies have more bones than adults.

  • The skeleton of a newborn baby has approximately 300 different components, which are a mixture of bones and cartilage. The cartilage eventually solidifies into bone in a process called ossification for example, the kneecaps of newborns start off as cartilage and become bone in a few years.

  •   Musculoskeletal System

  • The musculoskeletal system consists of all the bones, muscles, tendons, cartilage, joints and ligaments in a person's body. While the bones provide the body’s structure and support, the muscles supply the body’s movement abilities.
